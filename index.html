About Me

An Instructional Designer who loves a challenge, I enjoy helping people discover they can accomplish a lot more than they thought they could and I try to make learning new process and skills as painless as possible. I currently am creating courses with tenure faculty, GPTI instructors and adjuct instructors.

Some of the projects I've worked on include developing job aids, designing and creating processes for a variety of subjects including an online Speech course, an online Russian language course, online Ancient Greek language courses and an online dance course.  I have developed and implemented processes for adding measurable objectives to a development process, planned and implement a migration from one learning management system to another learning management system, and revision of processes because of continued growth of an online program. I have experience using a wide range of technologies and like to try new approaches and technologies. 

When I work with content experts or work on projects, I think that technology should be invisible so that the focus is on learning. Instruction should provide people with tools and skills that will help them be successful.

Planning and establishing objectives is a key part of developing instructional materials, but you also have to be ready to make adjustments if a plan isn't work or if the situation changes.

I try to keep things simple and look for ways that I can keep learning and apply what I learn in original ways. I feel that it is important focus on what you really want to accomplish on a project and those objectives should drive the direction and decisions throughout the entire project.
